## SECTION 1 — EXECUTIVE PRODUCT DEFINITION

### One-Sentence Product Definition

Kommison is a SaaS platform that gives small businesses a clean, modern system to run referral programs, track commissions, and pay partners — without spreadsheets or enterprise software.

### Elevator Pitch

Most small businesses run referrals informally — through DMs, memory, and messy spreadsheets. Someone refers a customer, a commission is owed, and nobody has a reliable record. Kommison replaces that chaos with a simple system: create referral partners, assign trackable links or codes, see every referred lead and conversion, and manage payouts from pending to paid. It's referral tracking built for businesses that are too busy for spreadsheets and too small for enterprise affiliate platforms.

### Market-Ready Description

Kommison is a referral and commission tracking platform designed for agencies, freelancers, consultants, and local service businesses. It lets you create a referral program in minutes, invite partners, assign unique referral links or codes, and automatically track every lead, conversion, and commission in one place. Partners get their own portal to see referrals and earnings. Business owners get a clean dashboard to review, approve, and manage payouts. No spreadsheets. No enterprise contracts. No bloat. Just a reliable system that makes referrals actually scalable.

### Who It's For

Small business owners who already get referrals but track them poorly — or not at all. Agencies paying referral fees to partners on handshake deals. Freelancers who want to formalize a referral program but don't want to wrestle with enterprise software. Local service businesses that know word-of-mouth drives revenue but have no infrastructure around it.

### What It Solves

The gap between "we have referral partners" and "we have a referral program." Most small businesses operate referrals on trust, memory, and inconsistent tracking. Kommison gives them a system — one that's lightweight enough to adopt in a day but structured enough to scale as revenue grows.

### Why Now

Three converging forces:

1. **The referral economy is growing but tooling hasn't caught up for SMBs.** Enterprise affiliate platforms (PartnerStack, Impact, Refersion) are overbuilt and overpriced for a 5-person agency. The spreadsheet-to-SaaS gap is wide open.
2. **Small businesses are more tool-savvy than ever.** Founders who use Stripe, Notion, and Linear expect the same quality in every tool they adopt. They'll pay $49/mo for something clean and reliable. They won't tolerate clunky dashboards or 90s-era UI.
3. **Referral revenue is the highest-trust, lowest-CAC channel for small businesses.** As ad costs rise and organic reach declines, formalizing referral programs becomes a growth strategy, not a nice-to-have.

### Why It's Smart

- **Low technical complexity, high operational value.** The core product is a CRUD app with a few smart workflows — not an AI model or a data pipeline. This means it can ship fast, run cheaply, and iterate quickly.
- **Clear willingness to pay.** Businesses already pay referral commissions; they just track them badly. Kommison doesn't create a new expense — it systematizes an existing one.
- **Natural network effects.** Every referral partner who receives an invite sees the product. Every partner portal login is a passive marketing touchpoint.
- **Compounding data moat.** Once a business has 6+ months of referral and commission data in Kommison, switching costs are real.

### Why It Fits the Portfolio

Kommison is the fourth product in the Bedrock Alliance ecosystem, and it fits precisely:

- **TweakAndBuild** is the agency that builds products like Kommison. Kommison becomes both a live case study and a client project showcase — proof that TweakAndBuild ships real, revenue-generating SaaS.
- **LeadsAndSaaS** handles the top of funnel (prospecting, outreach, lead gen). Kommison handles what happens after the deal — tracking the referral that brought the lead and paying the partner who sent it. These are complementary stages of the same revenue cycle.
- **Create3DParts** is a local service business — exactly the ICP for Kommison. It can use Kommison to run its own referral program, creating a real internal case study with real data.
- **Shared buyer persona.** All four products serve founders, freelancers, and small business owners who want professional-grade tools without enterprise overhead.

Kommison strengthens the portfolio's credibility: it shows range (agency, lead gen, local service, SaaS), it demonstrates that TweakAndBuild builds products that actually launch, and it creates internal cross-sell and case study opportunities that no single product could generate alone.

## SECTION 3 — IDEAL CUSTOMER PROFILE & USE CASES

### Core ICPs

**ICP 1: Small Agency Owner (Primary)**
- Runs a 1–15 person agency (marketing, dev, design, consulting)
- Revenue: $200K–$3M/year
- Already pays referral fees to partners — usually other agencies, freelancers, or past clients
- Tracks referrals in a spreadsheet, Notion table, or not at all
- Pays commissions via Venmo, PayPal, or check — manually, inconsistently
- Wants to look professional when partners ask "where's my referral at?"
- Tech-comfortable: uses Stripe, Slack, Linear, or similar tools daily
- Decision-maker is the founder or ops lead
- Will pay $29–$79/mo without a procurement process

**ICP 2: Freelancer / Solo Consultant**
- Independent operator: developer, designer, strategist, accountant, bookkeeper
- Revenue: $75K–$400K/year
- Has 3–15 informal referral partners (friends, past colleagues, other freelancers)
- Referral tracking is entirely in their head or a note-taking app
- Loses track of who referred whom, owes money to people and forgets
- Wants a system but doesn't want complexity
- Will pay $19–$39/mo for something that takes 10 minutes to set up

**ICP 3: Local Service Business**
- Home services, dental practices, real estate adjacent, insurance, auto shops
- Revenue: $300K–$5M/year
- Gets significant business from word-of-mouth
- May pay referral fees to other local businesses, realtors, or contractors
- Non-technical owner/manager — needs dead-simple UX
- Currently tracks nothing or uses a legal pad / basic spreadsheet
- Partner tracking is a compliance or tax concern (needs records)
- Will pay $39–$79/mo if it clearly replaces a painful manual process

### First Niche to Target and Why

**Target first: Small agency owners (ICP 1).**

Reasons:
1. **Highest pain, highest awareness.** They already pay referral commissions and know their tracking is broken. No market education needed.
2. **Accessible via TweakAndBuild's network.** The agency world is tight-knit. TweakAndBuild already operates in this space and can reach these buyers directly.
3. **Best case study material.** Agencies have clear, quantifiable referral flows (partner sends lead → lead converts to project → commission paid). Clean use case, easy to showcase.
4. **Natural virality.** When an agency invites a referral partner, that partner is often another agency or freelancer — another potential Kommison customer.
5. **Price-insensitive relative to value.** If an agency pays $2,000 in referral commissions per quarter, $49/mo for tracking is an obvious ROI.

### Top 10 Use Cases

1. **Agency referral program.** A marketing agency pays 10% commissions to partner agencies and freelancers who send clients. They use Kommison to assign unique referral codes, track which partners sent which leads, and manage quarterly payouts.

2. **Freelancer referral network.** A solo web developer has 8 colleagues who occasionally send work. They set up Kommison to give each a referral link, track conversions, and pay flat $200 fees per converted project.

3. **Client referral incentive.** A design studio offers existing clients a $500 credit for every new client they refer. Kommison tracks the referrals and credits, replacing a messy spreadsheet.

4. **Local service cross-referrals.** A home cleaning company and a real estate agent refer customers to each other. Both use Kommison to track referrals in both directions and settle commissions monthly.

5. **Sales team tracking.** A small SaaS company uses Kommison to track which team members or external sales partners referred which accounts, with percentage-based commissions on first-year revenue.

6. **Insurance referral tracking.** An insurance broker pays flat fees to auto shops and real estate agents who send referrals. Kommison tracks attributions and generates payout records for tax documentation.

7. **Consultant partner program.** A business consultant formalizes relationships with three accountants who refer business owners. Each gets a unique link, and the consultant pays $300 per engaged referral.

8. **Event / wedding vendor referrals.** A photographer pays commissions to wedding planners who refer clients. Kommison tracks which planner referred which couple and manages seasonal payout cycles.

9. **SaaS affiliate lite.** A bootstrapped SaaS founder wants a simple affiliate program for 10–20 partners without paying for PartnerStack. Kommison handles tracking and manual payouts.

10. **Contractor subcontractor referrals.** A general contractor pays referral bonuses to subcontractors who send new homeowner leads. Kommison tracks the referral chain and payout status.

## SECTION 4 — MVP DEFINITION

### Core MVP Promise

Give a small business owner a working referral program with partner tracking, lead attribution, commission calculation, and payout management — live in one day, useful from day one.

### What Must Ship in v1

- **Organization setup:** Sign up, create org, basic settings (company name, currency, default commission rate)
- **Referral program creation:** One program per org (multi-program is v2)
- **Partner management:** Add partners manually (name, email, company), edit, archive
- **Partner invite:** Send email invitation with onboarding link
- **Referral links:** Auto-generated unique referral link per partner
- **Referral codes:** Optional custom code per partner (e.g., "SARAH10")
- **Lead tracking:** Log referred leads (manually or via link click attribution)
- **Conversion tracking:** Mark a lead as converted (manual toggle)
- **Commission rules:** Flat fee or percentage per program (one rule per program in v1)
- **Commission calculation:** Auto-calculate commission when a conversion is logged
- **Payout management:** View pending commissions, approve, mark as paid
- **Partner portal:** Separate login for partners to view their referrals, conversions, and earnings
- **Basic dashboard:** Summary stats for admin (total referrals, conversions, commissions owed, commissions paid)
- **Activity log:** Simple event log (partner added, referral created, commission approved, payout marked)
- **Email notifications:** Partner invited, referral attributed, commission approved, payout completed

### What to Exclude from v1

- Stripe Connect or any automated payout processing
- Multi-program support (one program per org is sufficient for MVP)
- Public API
- Webhook integrations
- Zapier / Make integration
- Advanced analytics or reporting
- CSV import of existing partners or referrals
- Custom branding on partner portal
- Tiered or multi-level commission structures
- Recurring commission tracking
- Team member roles beyond single admin
- Embeddable referral widgets
- Referral link click analytics (beyond basic attribution)
- Custom domains for referral links

### What Belongs in v1.5 (1–3 months post-launch)

- CSV import/export for partners and referrals
- Multi-program support (multiple referral programs per org)
- Team members with basic roles (admin, viewer)
- Custom commission rules per partner (override program default)
- Basic reporting: referrals by source, conversion rate, top partners
- Custom branding on partner portal (logo, colors)
- Referral link click tracking and basic analytics
- Bulk actions (approve multiple payouts, archive multiple partners)

### What Belongs in v2 (3–6 months post-launch)

- Stripe Connect for automated payouts
- Public API for custom integrations
- Webhook support
- Zapier / Make integration
- Recurring commissions (for subscription-based businesses)
- Advanced analytics dashboard
- Embeddable referral widget for websites
- Custom domains for referral links
- Partner tiers (bronze, silver, gold with different rates)
- Multi-currency support
- White-label partner portal option
- Automated commission approval rules

### Where the Long-Term Moat Comes From

1. **Data lock-in.** Once a business has 12+ months of referral, conversion, and payout history in Kommison, migrating to a spreadsheet or another tool is painful. The data becomes the system of record.
2. **Partner network effects.** Every partner who logs into the portal sees the product. Partners who work with multiple businesses want them all on Kommison. The tool spreads through professional networks.
3. **Workflow habits.** Once the referral-to-payout workflow is embedded in how a business operates, switching costs are high — not because of contracts, but because of process dependency.
4. **Vertical depth.** Over time, Kommison can build features specific to niches (insurance compliance, real estate referral rules, agency project tracking) that horizontal tools can't match.
5. **Integration stickiness.** Once Stripe Connect, CRM integrations, and webhooks are live, Kommison sits in the middle of the business's revenue stack.

## SECTION 5 — PRODUCT FEATURES (STRUCTURED)

### 1. Referral Partner Management

**Purpose:** Central system for managing everyone who refers business to you.

**Why it matters:** Without a partner registry, referrals exist as informal relationships with no accountability. This is the foundation — if you can't manage partners, you can't track anything else.

**Minimum implementation (v1):**
- Add partner: name, email, company (optional), phone (optional), notes
- Edit partner details
- Archive partner (soft delete — preserves referral and commission history)
- Partner list view with search and status filter (active / archived)
- Partner detail page showing their referrals, conversions, and commissions
- One-click copy of partner's referral link

**Future expansion:**
- CSV import/export
- Partner tiers with different commission rates
- Partner tags and segments
- Partner performance scoring
- Bulk actions (invite, archive, update commission rate)
- Partner onboarding checklist

---

### 2. Referral Links and Codes

**Purpose:** Give each partner a unique, trackable identifier so referrals can be attributed correctly.

**Why it matters:** Attribution is the core value proposition. Without reliable links or codes, the business is back to guessing who referred whom.

**Minimum implementation (v1):**
- Auto-generated unique referral link per partner (e.g., `kommison.com/r/abc123` or custom domain later)
- Referral link redirects to a destination URL set by the business owner
- Optional custom referral code per partner (e.g., "SARAH" or "AGENCY10")
- Partner can copy their link/code from the partner portal
- Link click creates a referral record with source attribution
- Basic duplicate detection (same email/IP within 24 hours)

**Future expansion:**
- Custom domains for referral links
- QR code generation for each link
- Link click analytics (clicks, unique visitors, conversion rate)
- UTM parameter passthrough
- Embeddable referral widget
- Short link customization

---

### 3. Lead / Customer Attribution

**Purpose:** Connect a referred lead back to the partner who sent them.

**Why it matters:** This is where trust is built or broken. If attribution is inaccurate or opaque, partners lose confidence and stop referring. If it's clean and transparent, referrals increase.

**Minimum implementation (v1):**
- Referral created automatically when someone clicks a referral link (captures: name/email if provided, referral source, timestamp, partner ID)
- Manual referral entry by admin (for phone referrals, in-person intros, etc.)
- Referral status workflow: `new` → `contacted` → `converted` / `lost`
- Each referral clearly linked to one partner
- Admin can reassign or edit referral attribution
- Referral detail view with full history

**Future expansion:**
- Cookie-based attribution with configurable window (7/14/30/90 days)
- Multi-touch attribution (first touch vs. last touch)
- Automatic CRM sync (pull lead status from external CRM)
- Form embed that captures referral code at point of submission
- Webhook to receive referral events from external systems
- Duplicate lead detection and merge

---

### 4. Commission Logic

**Purpose:** Define how much partners earn per referral or conversion, and calculate it automatically.

**Why it matters:** Manual commission calculation is where most spreadsheet-based systems break. Wrong amounts, forgotten bonuses, and inconsistent rates erode partner trust. Automated, rule-based calculation eliminates this.

**Minimum implementation (v1):**
- Program-level commission rule: flat fee OR percentage
- Flat fee: fixed dollar amount per conversion (e.g., $200 per converted lead)
- Percentage: percentage of deal value (requires admin to enter deal value when marking conversion)
- Commission auto-calculated when a referral is marked as converted
- Admin can manually override commission amount on any individual referral
- Commission states: `pending` → `approved` → `paid`

**Future expansion:**
- Per-partner commission rate overrides
- Tiered commissions (e.g., first 5 referrals at 10%, next 10 at 12%)
- Recurring commissions (for subscription revenue — percentage of monthly recurring)
- Bonus rules (e.g., $100 bonus for 10+ referrals in a quarter)
- Commission caps (maximum per referral or per period)
- Multi-currency commission rules

---

### 5. Payout Tracking

**Purpose:** Track which commissions have been paid and which are outstanding.

**Why it matters:** This is the financial accountability layer. Without it, business owners can't answer "how much do I owe my partners?" and partners can't answer "have I been paid?" That ambiguity kills referral programs.

**Minimum implementation (v1):**
- Payout status per commission: `pending` → `approved` → `paid`
- Admin approves individual commissions (moves from pending to approved)
- Admin marks commissions as paid (with optional payment reference / note)
- Payouts dashboard: filterable by status (pending, approved, paid), partner, and date range
- Per-partner payout summary (total owed, total paid, balance)
- Basic payout history with timestamps

**Future expansion:**
- Stripe Connect automated payouts
- Batch payout processing (pay all approved commissions for a partner in one action)
- Payout scheduling (auto-approve on the 1st of each month)
- Payment method tracking per partner (PayPal, bank transfer, Venmo, check)
- Payout receipts / statements (downloadable PDF per partner per period)
- Tax document support (1099 tracking for US partners)

---

### 6. Partner Portal

**Purpose:** Give referral partners a dedicated, self-service view of their referrals, conversions, and earnings.

**Why it matters:** Partner transparency is the single biggest driver of referral volume. When partners can see their referrals and earnings in real time, they refer more. When they can't, they assume they're being shortchanged and stop.

**Minimum implementation (v1):**
- Separate login experience for partners (email + password, magic link)
- Dashboard: total referrals, conversions, earnings summary (pending / approved / paid)
- Referral link prominently displayed with one-click copy
- List of their referrals with status (new, contacted, converted, lost)
- Earnings breakdown: pending commissions, approved (awaiting payment), paid
- No ability to edit business data — read-only with link sharing

**Future expansion:**
- Custom branding (business logo, colors on partner portal)
- Partner profile editing (update contact info, payment preferences)
- Referral submission form (partner can submit a lead directly)
- Performance insights (their conversion rate, rank among partners)
- Document sharing (agreements, tax forms)
- White-label portal option

---

### 7. Notifications

**Purpose:** Keep both admins and partners informed about key referral events without requiring them to check the dashboard constantly.

**Why it matters:** Timely notifications drive action. An admin notified of a new referral can follow up fast. A partner notified of a conversion stays motivated. Silence kills engagement.

**Minimum implementation (v1):**
- Email notifications only (no in-app notifications in v1)
- Admin receives: new referral attributed, new partner accepted invite
- Partner receives: invitation to join, referral marked as converted, commission approved, payout marked as paid
- All emails are transactional, plain, and professional (no marketing fluff)
- Unsubscribe option on non-critical notifications

**Future expansion:**
- In-app notification center
- Notification preferences (per event type, per channel)
- Slack integration for admin alerts
- Weekly digest email for partners (your referrals this week)
- Webhook notifications for external systems
- SMS notifications for high-value events

---

### 8. Audit Trail / Activity Log

**Purpose:** Maintain a chronological record of every significant action in the system.

**Why it matters:** Referral tracking involves money. When disputes arise — "I sent that referral last month" or "you never approved my commission" — the activity log is the source of truth. It protects both the business and the partner.

**Minimum implementation (v1):**
- System-generated log entries for: partner created/archived, referral created/updated, conversion logged, commission calculated/approved/overridden, payout marked as paid
- Each entry: timestamp, actor (admin or system), action, affected entity, old value → new value (where applicable)
- Activity log viewable on admin dashboard (recent activity widget)
- Activity log viewable on partner detail page (actions related to that partner)
- Not editable or deletable by any user

**Future expansion:**
- Filterable global activity log (by event type, date range, actor, partner)
- Exportable audit trail (CSV/PDF for compliance or tax purposes)
- Activity log on individual referral detail pages
- Partner-visible activity feed (limited to their own referrals)
- Retention policies and archiving

---

### 9. Settings

**Purpose:** Central configuration for the business's referral program and account.

**Why it matters:** Settings need to be minimal in v1 but cover the essentials. Overbuilding settings is a classic MVP trap — keep it lean.

**Minimum implementation (v1):**
- Company info: name, logo, website URL
- Program settings: program name, description, destination URL (where referral links redirect)
- Commission defaults: type (flat/percentage), rate, currency
- Account settings: admin email, password change
- Danger zone: delete account (with confirmation)

**Future expansion:**

### 10. Analytics

**Purpose:** Help the business owner understand referral program performance and make data-driven decisions.

**Why it matters:** Analytics turn Kommison from a tracking tool into a growth tool. But in v1, the analytics need to be minimal and actionable — not a BI dashboard.

**Minimum implementation (v1):**
- Dashboard summary cards: total partners (active), total referrals (this month / all time), total conversions (this month / all time), conversion rate, total commissions pending, total commissions paid
- Top partners list (by referrals and by conversions)
- Simple trend: referrals this month vs. last month (directional arrow, not a chart)

**Future expansion:**
- Time-series charts (referrals, conversions, commissions over time)
- Partner leaderboard
- Conversion funnel visualization
- Revenue attributed to referrals
- Cohort analysis (partner performance over time)
- Export reports (CSV, PDF)
- Scheduled report emails (weekly/monthly)
- ROI calculator (commissions paid vs. revenue generated)
